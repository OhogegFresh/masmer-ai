Week 1 — Legal & Entity
0 / 6
Priority this week: Get your legal foundation locked before anything else moves. The LLC vs C-corp decision affects how investors come in, how equity splits, and how you protect yourselves. Founders agreement before any work splits further.
Entity Formation
Research LLC vs C-corp for Masmer AI
Omer Critical
Compare tax implications, investor compatibility (VCs strongly prefer C-corp Delaware), liability protection, and complexity. C-corp is almost always the move if you're raising.
Make entity decision & begin filing
Both Critical
Once decided, initiate incorporation. Delaware C-corp is the standard for funded startups - takes 1-3 days online via Stripe Atlas, Clerky, or a lawyer.
Founders Agreement
Draft founders agreement (Omer & Eitan)
Both Critical
Cover equity split, vesting schedule (4yr / 1yr cliff), roles, IP assignment, decision-making authority, and what happens if one founder exits. Use a lawyer or Clerky template.
Sign founders agreement
Both Critical
Both parties sign. Use DocuSign or a notarized copy. This is your foundation - nothing else is safe until this is signed.
Legal & Liability
Map out legal liability framework
Both Important
Identify: what data you collect from contractors, what data contractors collect from their customers (homeowners), and your exposure. Consider CCPA/GDPR implications for SaaS storing homeowner data.
Draft ToS & Privacy Policy for Masmer AI platform
Both Important
Protects you from contractor disputes and covers how homeowner data (passed through by contractors) is handled. Must cover liability if the AI gives a bad estimate.

Market Research
Run TAM/SAM/SOM analysis for Masmer AI

Validate demand: survey or interview 10 contractors

Week 4 — Fundraising
0 / 5
End goal: Walk into any investor meeting knowing exactly how much you need, what it buys, and what your cap table looks like after. Never ask for money without a budget. The budget IS the ask.
Budget & Ask
Build 18-month operating budget
Both Critical
Line-item: salaries/contractor costs, VAPI usage, Supabase/hosting, legal fees, marketing spend, and buffer. 18 months of runway is the standard ask. Know your monthly burn rate cold.
Determine fundraising ask & use-of-funds breakdown
Both Critical
Tie every dollar to a milestone. "We need $X to hire 2 engineers, close 50 paying customers, and file IP." Investors fund outcomes, not operations. Build a use-of-funds slide from your budget.
Update cap table with option pool & pre-seed scenario
Both Critical
Run the option pool shuffle: investors will require 10-15% ESOP carved from founders before they calculate their stake. Model pre/post-money dilution for $250K, $500K, and $1M scenarios using the Masmer cap table calculator.
Investor Outreach
Build target investor list (20+ names)
Omer Important
Focus on: pre-seed funds ($100K-$1M check size), proptech/construction tech angels, and operators who've built in home services. AngelList, Crunchbase, LinkedIn. Warm intros beat cold outreach 10:1.
Update pitch deck with market research & budget data
Both Important
Pull in your TAM numbers, contractor survey results, and use-of-funds slide. The deck needs: problem, solution, market size, traction, team, ask. "Built by a contractor" is the opening line - lead with it.

The goal: a working product, a signed team, and a number.
