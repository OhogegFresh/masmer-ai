Masmer AI — UX/UI Design Brief

📄 Version 1.0 · June 2026 ✍️ Prepared by Omer (Founder) 👤 For: UX/UI Designer

Overview

Masmer AI is a CRM and operations platform built specifically for contractors and home service companies. It replaces the chaos of missed calls, paper estimates, and manual follow-ups with an AI-powered system that handles those things automatically.

The product has three core systems that connect in a loop:

  1. AI Receptionist — answers missed calls, captures leads, schedules callbacks
  2. CRM Pipeline — manages customers, jobs, and projects from lead to payment
  3. AI Estimate Bot — generates professional estimates from a plain-English job description

Your job is to design the full app UI — every screen, every state, every flow — so the engineering team can build directly from your files.

Users

Primary user: A contractor or small home service business owner. Think 1–5 person crew. They're on job sites, on their phone, usually not sitting at a desk. They're not technical. They want things fast and obvious.

Secondary user: An office manager or admin (optional for V1 — design with this in mind but don't optimize for it).

Design Direction

  • Dark UI. The app is dark-first. Background: #0D1117. Cards: #111827.
  • Orange accent. Primary action color: #F97316. Used for CTAs, active states, key numbers.
  • Clean and confident. No clutter. Every screen should feel like a tool, not a dashboard overloaded with widgets.
  • Mobile-aware. The app lives on desktop and tablet primarily, but key flows (call log, schedule, customer lookup) need to work on mobile.
  • Typography: Syne (headings/display) + DM Sans (body). These are already decided — please use them.

Screens to Design

1. Dashboard (Home)

Purpose: Morning glance. What's happening today, what needs attention.

Key elements:

  • Top stat row: Active Customers, Revenue MTD, Active Projects, Missed Calls (handled by AI)
  • Today's schedule — 3–5 upcoming jobs/calls in a list
  • AI Receptionist activity feed — recent calls the AI handled
  • Lead pipeline visual — simple bar or funnel showing New → Quoted → Active → Closed
  • Quick Actions grid — shortcuts to: New Estimate, Add Customer, Schedule Job, Create Invoice

States to design: Default (data populated) · Empty state (new account, no data yet)

2. Customers

Purpose: The main CRM list. Every lead and customer lives here.

  • Search + filter bar at top (filter by status: Lead / Active / Completed / All)
  • View toggle: Table view ↔ Kanban pipeline view
  • Table view columns: Name, Status badge, Phone, Location, Last Job, Total Value, Action button
  • Kanban view columns: New Lead → Quoted → Active Job → Completed
  • Each kanban card shows: Name, job type, value, source (AI lead / referral / web)
  • "Add Customer" button (opens a modal/drawer)

States: Table with data · Empty state per kanban column · Search with no results

3. Customer Detail Page

Critical screen — this needs to feel like a complete record. Everything about one customer in one place.

Layout: Left column (contact info + notes) + right column (tabbed detail panel)

Left column: Avatar/initials · Name, email, phone, address · Status badge · Total jobs + revenue · Notes field (editable) · Quick actions: Call, Message, New Job

Right column — tabs:

  • Jobs — list of all jobs (name, date, status, value)
  • Estimates — estimates sent (status: pending / accepted / expired)
  • Invoices — invoices issued (status: unpaid / paid / overdue)
  • Activity — chronological log of everything

4. Schedule

Purpose: See all jobs and appointments on a calendar. Book new ones.

Views to design: Month (primary) · Week · List

Event color coding:

  • 🟠 Orange = Estimate appointment
  • 🔵 Blue = Active job / site visit
  • 🟢 Green = Follow-up / call
  • 🟡 Amber = Invoice due

Sidebar panel (month view): Today's appointments as a stacked list with time, customer name, job type, location.

States: Calendar with events · Empty week/month · "Add Job" modal (customer, job type, date/time, location, notes)

5. Projects

Purpose: Track active jobs from start to finish.

  • Stat row: Active, In-Flight Value, Due This Week, Completed YTD
  • Table: Project name, customer, start date, est. end date, progress bar (%), value, status badge
  • Status badges: Starting / On Track / Mid-Job / Finishing / Completed / Delayed
  • Color-coded left border per project (matches schedule color)

Project detail: Progress tracker / phases · Linked estimate + invoice · Notes / log · Photos (placeholder slot for V1)

6. Estimates

Purpose: All estimates in one place. Send, track, manage.

  • Stat row: Open value, Accepted, Pending, Expired
  • Table: Estimate #, Customer, Job, Date Sent, Expiry, Status, Total
  • Status badges: Pending / Accepted / Expired / Draft
  • Filter by status

Estimate detail: Line-item breakdown · Labor + materials split · Markup shown · Download PDF, Send to Customer, Convert to Invoice

7. Invoices

Purpose: Track what's been billed and what's been paid.

  • Stat row: Collected MTD, Outstanding, Overdue, Avg Days to Pay
  • Table: Invoice #, Customer, Job, Issued, Due, Status, Amount
  • Status badges: Unpaid / Paid / Overdue / Partial
  • Overdue rows should be visually distinct (red left border or red text)

Invoice detail: Line items · Payment history · Send reminder button · Mark as paid button

8. Expenses

Purpose: Log and track job costs to understand margins.

  • Stat row: Spent MTD, Gross Margin %, Materials total, Sub-Contractor total
  • Table: Date, Description, Category (Materials / Sub / Equipment / Other), Project, Receipt (yes/no), Amount
  • Categories shown as color-coded badges

9. AI Estimate Bot

Purpose: The contractor describes a job in plain English, the AI outputs a full estimate.

Layout: Full-page chat interface

  • Chat history (bot messages on left, user on right)
  • Bot opens with a clear prompt: "Tell me about the job — what's the work, where is it, who's the customer?"
  • Suggestion chips at start: Bathroom remodel / Kitchen reno / Deck build / Interior paint / Flooring / Custom
  • After user describes the job: structured estimate card (line items, subtotals, markup, total) + action buttons: Save Estimate / Download PDF / Email to Customer
  • Input bar at bottom: multiline text field + send button
  • "New Estimate" button clears chat and resets
Important: The estimate output card will be seen by customers — it needs to look professional and clean, not like a chat bubble.

10. AI Receptionist

Purpose: Show the contractor everything the AI handled — calls, leads captured, follow-ups sent.

  • Status pill at top: Online / Offline (green dot when active)
  • Stat row: Calls Handled, Leads Captured, Awaiting Review, Answer Rate
  • Call log: Inbound / Outbound / Missed icons · Caller name · AI summary (1 line) · Time + duration · Badge
  • Filter tabs: All / Missed / Leads / AI Handled
  • Lead card (highlighted): AI summary of what the caller said + Quick actions: Call Back / Build Estimate / Add to CRM

11. Settings

Sections: Business Profile · AI Receptionist config · Estimates defaults · Invoices & Payments · Plan & Billing

Modals / Drawers Needed

  • Add Customer — name, phone, email, address, status, source, notes
  • Add Job / Schedule — customer (search), job type, date, time, location, notes
  • Add Expense — date, description, category, project, amount, receipt upload
  • Create Invoice — customer, linked estimate (optional), line items, due date

Key Flows to Map

  1. New lead → estimate → job → invoice (the core loop)
  2. AI answers a call → lead captured → contractor reviews → builds estimate
  3. Contractor describes a job in Estimate Bot → estimate generated → sent to customer
  4. New customer added manually → job scheduled → project tracked

Empty States

Every section needs an empty state. Don't leave blank tables. Each empty state needs:

  • An icon or illustration
  • A 1-sentence explanation of what goes here
  • A clear CTA button (e.g. "Add your first customer")

What We're NOT Building in V1

  • No team / multi-user (single contractor account only)
  • No time tracking
  • No material takeoffs or detailed scope-of-work builder
  • No client portal (customer-facing view)
  • No reporting / analytics charts beyond the stat cards
Note: These are on the roadmap — design with them in mind but don't block on them.

Deliverables Expected

  • Full screen designs in Figma (desktop-first, mobile for key flows)
  • Component library / design system file (colors, typography, buttons, badges, inputs, cards)
  • Annotated flows for the 4 key user journeys above
  • Handoff-ready specs (spacing, states, hover, focus, disabled)

Questions? Reach out first.

Don't make major layout decisions without checking in. Happy to jump on a quick call to clarify anything before you go deep on a screen.

Masmer AI — Design Sprint Brief

🎯 Version 1.1 · June 2026 ✍️ Prepared by Omer (Founder) 🚀 First Sprint — 3 Priority Screens
Purpose: This is our first sprint together — a chance to test how we work. These 3 screens are the heart of the product. Nail these and the rest of the app follows their pattern.

Context

This is not a full app brief. We're zooming in on the 3 most important surfaces in Masmer. Every other screen can wait. These 3 need to feel world-class because they're what a contractor will live inside every single day.

The users driving this: salespeople and project managers who spend all day in their car, hopping between leads and job sites. The UI needs to work for someone who's glancing at their phone between stops, not someone sitting at a desk.

1
Customer Profile
Sidebar / Detail View — the contractor's complete file on every customer

What This Is

When a contractor taps on a customer anywhere in the app, a full customer profile opens. Think of it as the contractor's complete file on that person - everything they need to know, everything they can do, all in one place.

This is the most information-dense screen in the app. It needs to be dense and scannable - not overwhelming.

Layout

Recommended: Right-side slide-over panel (like a drawer) that opens over the customer list - OR a dedicated full page. Designer's call. But it must feel fast to open and close.

Section 1 - Header (Always Visible)

Sits at the top and never scrolls away.

  • Customer avatar (initials + background color, auto-generated)
  • Full name (large)
  • Phone number — tappable to call on mobile
  • Address — tappable to open maps
  • Status badge: Lead / Quoted / Active / Completed / Archived
  • Quick action buttons (always visible): 📞 Call · ✉️ Follow-up · 📋 New Estimate · 🗓 Schedule Job · ➕ Add Expense

Section 2 - Key Dates (Prominent, Right Under Header)

These are critical for a contractor and constantly referenced. Do not bury them in a tab.

FieldDescription
First Visit DateWhen the salesperson first went out to this lead
Estimate SentDate the estimate was sent to the customer
Follow-up DueNext scheduled follow-up (editable)
Job Start DateWhen work began (if applicable)
Job End / CompletionWhen job was completed

Each date is editable inline — tap to change. If a date hasn't been set yet, show a clear "Set date" prompt, not a blank space.

Section 3 - Job Notes

Free-text notes field. The salesperson or PM writes what the customer wants, scope details from the first visit, preferences, quirks, property specifics.

Must have: Auto-save (no save button needed). Timestamped so they know when it was last edited.

Section 4 - Photos

  • Photo grid (2–3 columns)
  • Tap to upload (camera or library on mobile, file picker on desktop)
  • Tap photo to view full size
  • Optional: label/caption per photo
  • Upload date shown on each thumbnail

Empty state: "No photos yet — tap to add from your camera or files."

Section 5 - Tabbed Detail Panel

Tab: Estimates — List of all estimates with: Estimate #, scope label, date sent, status badge (Draft / Sent / Pending / Accepted / Declined / Expired), total amount, actions (View / Resend / Convert to Invoice). Prominent "Create New Estimate" button at top that opens the Estimate Bot with this customer pre-selected.

Tab: Invoices — Invoice #, linked job, issue + due date, status (Unpaid / Paid / Overdue / Partial), amount, actions. Overdue invoices visually flagged with red left border or red text.

Tab: Payments — What's been collected. Date paid, amount, method (Cash / Check / Card / Zelle / Other), linked invoice, notes. Running lifetime total at bottom.

Tab: Job Expenses — Expenses tied to this specific customer/job (separate from company-wide Expenses). Columns: Date, Description, Category (Materials / Sub-Contractor / Equipment / Permit / Other), Amount, Receipt attached.

Inline add: Tap "+ Add Expense" and a quick-add row appears inline — no modal. Just: Description, Category, Amount, Receipt. Keep it fast.

Summary bar at top of this tab: Total Materials · Total Sub/Labor · Total Expenses · Est. Margin % (estimate total minus expenses)

Tab: Activity Log — Chronological feed, auto-generated. Icon + plain English description + timestamp. Most recent at top. Feels like a CRM timeline — clean, compact, scannable.

Follow-up Flow

1
Tap "Send Follow-up" from the customer profile
2
Choose method: SMS / Email (WhatsApp future V2)
3
See a pre-written AI-drafted message (editable before sending). Pulls in customer name, job type from notes/estimate, prompt to move forward.
4
Send — activity log auto-records it

States to Design

  • Full profile (data populated across all tabs)
  • New customer (just created — dates not set, no estimates, notes empty)
  • Lead with no estimate yet
  • Customer with overdue invoice (payment tab flagged)
  • Archived customer
2
Schedule
Simple, fast, mobile-first — built for people driving between jobs all day
Core principle: The people using this screen are in their car all day. Keep it as simple as humanly possible. No clutter. Fast to read. Fast to act.

Four Views

ViewPurpose
DayToday's jobs only. Default view when Schedule opens.
Week7-day grid. Quick overview of the week.
MonthFull calendar. Spot open/busy days at a glance.
ListUpcoming jobs sorted by date. Cleanest for on-the-go.

Day View

  • Vertical timeline, ~7am to 7pm
  • Each job is a card/block on the timeline
  • Tap a job → popup opens
  • Empty time slots visible but minimal — don't clutter
  • Top line: date + "X jobs today"
  • Floating "+" button to add a new job

Week View

  • 7 columns (Sun–Sat or Mon–Sun)
  • Each day shows compact job chips (customer name + time)
  • Tap chip → popup opens · Tap day header → switches to Day view

Month View

  • Standard calendar grid
  • Days with jobs show colored dots or chips
  • 🟠 Orange = Estimate / First visit · 🔵 Blue = Active job · 🟢 Green = Follow-up · 🟡 Amber = Invoice due
  • Tap a day → expands or switches to Day view

List View

Simplest view. Sorted by date ascending. Each row: Date + day label, Time, Customer name, Job type, Status badge, arrow to open popup. Group by date. Today separated clearly from upcoming.

3
AI Estimate Bot
A conversation that ends with a professional document — ready to send
Core principle: This should feel like texting a really smart estimator — not filling out a form. The contractor describes the job, the AI asks questions, builds a materials list, confirms the price, and generates a quote they can send directly from the chat.

Layout

Full-page chat interface. On desktop: optional two-column layout — chat on left, live estimate preview building on the right. On mobile: single column, estimate preview collapses below the chat.

The Conversation — 5 Steps

1
Contractor describes the job — types or taps a suggestion chip. Example: "Bathroom remodel for a customer in Brooklyn. Subway tile, new vanity, frameless shower door."
2
AI asks clarifying questions — one at a time. "What's the approximate square footage?" — "Full demo or existing walls?" — "Plumbing rough-in included or a separate sub?" — Max 3–5 questions. Feels like a conversation, not an interrogation.
3
AI generates a materials list. Structured output: materials by item, labor breakdown by task. Contractor can reply to adjust — "Add heated floor mat" — and the list updates.
4
AI asks for the final price. "What do you want to charge the customer? I can suggest a range based on typical rates if you'd like." If they ask, AI gives low / mid / high with brief explanation.
5
AI generates the full quote card — professional, formatted, with line items, subtotals, markup, total, payment terms, and action buttons inline.

This card will be emailed/texted to the customer. It must look professional and clean — not like a chat bubble. Design it as a polished document, not a system message.

Suggestion Chips (Fresh Open)

When no conversation exists, show quick-start chips: 🚿 Bathroom remodel · 🍳 Kitchen renovation · 🌿 Deck / outdoor · 🎨 Interior paint · 🏠 Flooring · 🔨 Custom job

States to Design

  • Fresh open — no conversation, suggestion chips visible
  • Mid-conversation — AI asking questions, estimate not yet generated
  • Materials list generated — waiting for price confirmation
  • Full estimate generated — all action buttons showing
  • Estimate linked to customer — customer name shows at top of quote card
  • Estimate sent — confirmation state ("✓ Sent to Jose Martinez")
  • Mobile layout — single column, estimate below chat

Notes for the Designer

  • Every screen needs empty states — don't leave blanks.
  • Every interactive element needs hover, focus, and active states.
  • Phone numbers and addresses are always tappable — this is mobile-critical even on desktop.
  • The Job Popup on Schedule is the most mobile-critical element in the whole app. It needs to work perfectly on a phone screen.
  • The Estimate Bot quote card will be seen by customers — professional and clean, not a chat bubble.
  • When in doubt: simpler is better. Every extra tap is friction for someone between job sites.

Questions? Reach out before going deep.
